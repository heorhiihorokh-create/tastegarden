# LLM Council — Taste Garden design audit (2026-06-20)

**Question:** Is the current site best-in-class / 2026-quality, or "AI slop"? What concretely must change?

## The Contrarian
Single most damaging flaw is the photography — decent stock + a fake sushi shot (Asian table standing in for sushi) is a counterfeit; 2026 audiences sense it instantly and no motion rescues it. AI-slop tells: gradient-on-dark-photo hero + ember particles + clip-mask stagger headline (most templated combo of 2023–25); count-up numbers everywhere (animating "€43" is absurd, kill it); dark glass cards + featured pricing card + chips (SaaS muscle memory); parallax on every band (nothing reads as intentional); Playfair + Inter (most-defaulted "luxury" pairing). Plaque + gilt frame + corner brackets + swaying lanterns = three kitsch signals stacked → "Chinatown takeaway 2009", not premium. Lacks a single human, a real chef, live teppanyaki fire, one genuine point of view.

## The First Principles Thinker
Wrong target. A family in Izegem at 18:00 wants four answers fast — is the food good, what does it cost, when are you open, can I book tonight — and to feel safe spending on a group meal. Measure against appetite, clarity, trust, speed. Dark cinematic theme mutes the exact thing that creates hunger (light, color, steam, abundance); "dark & moody" signals fine-dining tasting menus, not à-volonté family buffets — that mismatch is the real slop tell. Ember canvas, swaying lanterns, magnetic buttons, parallax, count-ups cost load/CPU on mid-range Android, delay the food, add zero booking intent. Priority: 1) real art-directed food photography (steam, fullness, live flame); 2) price/hours/location/"book tonight" without scrolling; 3) fast on a €200 phone (LCP < 2.5s, kill canvas); 4) trust (reviews, real room, allergens, kids' pricing); 5) restrained motion.

## The Expansionist
Signature spine: "the garden grows at the speed of your scroll" — the page is one continuous garden that germinates as you descend. Concrete moments: (1) ember-to-bloom hero — embers rise/cool/land as glowing seeds that sprout sprigs along the headline, swirling toward the cursor; (2) teppanyaki heat-haze on hold/hover (WebGL shimmer + sizzle + sear mark), each station with its own sense (wok wind-ripple, sushi koi crossing, dessert sugar-frost); (3) "build your plate" à-volonté abundance moment (drag dishes onto a plate that fills/tilts); (4) an unbroken vine/light-thread connecting sections. Kill all stock; commission one art-directed flame-and-steam macro set; the koi/vine/ember become the ownable, un-templatable signature.

## The Outsider (55-yr-old, on phone, outside)
"Is this place open, or a nightclub?" Everything black with thin gold writing; gold-on-black fancy italic is hard to read, has to tilt the screen. Lanterns/sparks cute for two seconds then feel busy/slow. Worst part: slippery momentum scroll — flick once and it slides past the prices, overshoots twice. Biggest annoyance: taps "Menu" wanting the FOOD, gets a list of links — where are the dishes? Likes: "Lunch 25, Diner 43, drinks included" is clear, photos look tasty, kids' prices help, today-highlighted hours good. Trust shaky — feels like a flashy ad, wants a readable address, tappable phone, a normal photo of the place. Language switch hidden in Menu — French-speaking sister would never find it. Would bounce and just phone them.

## The Executor — ordered build plan
Quick wins (≤1h): Restaurant JSON-LD structured data; OG/social image + metadata; sushi photo swap (art-directed); reservation success/error states + honeypot. Medium (1–4h): wire reservation email (Resend/route handler + Zod, owner + autoreply); real FOOD menu page `/menu`; cohesive imagery pass (one warm palette). Motion: KEEP hero ember + masked headline, dishes bento parallax; ADD one SplitText section-entry reveal; CUT magnetic buttons, second count-up, swaying lanterns if they cost FPS. Skip polish-theater: watermarks, Lenis tuning beyond defaults, micro-anims nobody sees.

## Chairman synthesis
Agree: imagery is #1; cut animation clichés; dark must make food glow not mute it; AA contrast for 18–70+; ship booking + schema + OG (decorative until booking works). Clash: bold signature moments vs less-motion/more-clarity → resolve via craft + art direction + ONE restrained signature. Blind spot: "Menu" ≠ food menu; hidden language switch. Recommendation: reframe to appetite-first/fast/trustworthy, dark only to spotlight food; keep Asian identity but refine (no kitsch). First: fix imagery (sushi + cohesive warm grade).
